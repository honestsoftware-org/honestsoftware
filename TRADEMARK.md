This is the **movement-level** Trademark Policy. It governs marks of
the HONEST movement itself. *Reference implementations* (such as
VaultAI) maintain their own product-level trademark policies for their
product-specific marks; this document does not govern those.

---

## 1. Why a trademark policy at all

A movement that licenses code permissively (or even semi-permissively)
under filial-duty conditions has exactly one durable defense against
appropriation by extractive operators: **the names and marks under which
the movement signals its identity**.

Code can be forked. License clauses can be argued in court. But "this
product is built under the HONEST Manifesto" and "this software is
HONEST-compliant" are *claims of identity*. If a bad-faith operator can
make those claims while violating the principles, the movement loses its
ability to mean anything. The trademark policy exists to prevent that
specific failure mode.

This is not a defensive crouch; it is *infrastructure for honesty*. It
ensures that when someone reads "HONEST-compliant" on a product, they
can trust that the operator has agreed to be measured by the same
principles as everyone else who uses that phrase.

## 3. What use of the marks requires permission

**Permission is required** to:

- Apply any reserved mark (above) to a *commercial product, service,
  hosted offering, or paid distribution* you operate. Permission is
  granted only if your offering is in good standing under the HONEST
  License and has a current published Filial Duty Statement (per
  [`LICENSE-CONCEPT.md`](./LICENSE-CONCEPT.md) §5).
- Use any reserved mark in a way that suggests endorsement, sponsorship,
  certification, or affiliation by the HONEST movement, the maintainer-
  collective, or any reference implementation, when no such relationship
  exists.
- Register, file, or claim any of the reserved marks (or any
  confusingly-similar mark) in any jurisdiction.
- Sell merchandise (apparel, stickers, books, swag) bearing any reserved
  mark or logo.
- Use any reserved logo in any context.

**Permission is NOT required** to:

- Refer to the marks accurately in editorial, journalistic, academic,
  educational, comparative, critical, or descriptive contexts. *"Project
  X follows the HONEST Manifesto"*, *"This is not HONEST-compliant"*, or
  *"The HONEST movement is wrong about Y"* — all are uses we explicitly
  do not gate. Honest criticism of the movement is welcome and protected.
- State, in personal or non-commercial use, that your own software
  follows the HONEST Manifesto or honors Inviolata, when it actually
  does. Self-declaration by individuals, hobbyists, students, families,
  schools, libraries, charities, and similar non-commercial actors is
  welcome and ungated.
- Quote or excerpt the Manifesto, Inviolata Principle, Succession
  design, or License Concept in writing, with attribution. All four are
  licensed CC BY 4.0 specifically to enable this.
- Fork the source code under the terms of the HONEST License (when v1.0
  exists), as long as you do not claim that your fork is the canonical
  HONEST project.

The principle: **factual reference is free; identity-claiming is gated.**

## 4. The "HONEST-compliant" mark in particular

The phrase **HONEST-compliant** is the most consequential mark in this
policy, because it is the one that connects an outside reader to a trust
signal. The intended policy:

- Any *individual or non-commercial actor* may self-declare HONEST
  compliance for software they operate, on the strength of their own
  Filial Duty Statement, without applying for permission.
- Any *commercial or institutional operator* who wishes to apply
  "HONEST-compliant" to a paid or commercially-distributed product or
  service must have a published Filial Duty Statement and must be in
  good standing under the HONEST License (when v1.0 exists). No central
  audit; no certification fee; just a public commitment that the operator
  agrees to be measured by.
- An operator found to violate the principles (per the cure-and-
  termination process in [`LICENSE-CONCEPT.md`](./LICENSE-CONCEPT.md) §6)
  loses the right to use "HONEST-compliant" as part of license
  termination. Continued use after termination is a trademark violation.

This is deliberately the lightest possible mechanism: no committee, no
fee, no audit. The honor system, with the trademark as the backstop when
the honor system fails.

## 5. The "Inviolata" mark in particular

Per [`INVIOLATA.md`](./INVIOLATA.md), a project may claim to honor the
Inviolata Principle when it implements all seven architectural commitments
and pre-commits publicly to all the political commitments declared there.

A project that introduces a feature violating any of the architectural
commitments **must immediately drop the Inviolata claim** until the
feature is removed. Public misrepresentation — claiming Inviolata while
shipping a backdoor, key escrow, exceptional-access mechanism, or any
equivalent — is a trademark violation under this policy and a license
violation under [`LICENSE-CONCEPT.md`](./LICENSE-CONCEPT.md) §4.9.

## 6. The maintainer-collective entity

This trademark policy presumes the existence of a maintainer-collective
entity (foundation, LLC, or similar) to hold the marks and act as
licensor. **No such entity exists yet.**

Until the entity is formed:

- The marks are held *in good-faith trust* by the original maintainer
  ([@sgireddy](https://github.com/sgireddy)).
- The maintainer commits, in writing, to transferring the marks to the
  maintainer-collective entity at the time of formation, on terms
  consistent with this policy.
- The marks will not be transferred, sold, sublicensed, or used as
  collateral in any commercial transaction prior to that formation.
- If the maintainer becomes unavailable, the marks shall be released
  into a community trust rather than transferred to any commercial
  entity.

This is *not* the long-term posture; it is the bridge between Day 1 and
the entity formation. Forming the entity is on the roadmap (see §8).

## 8. Roadmap

In rough priority order, the open trademark work is:

1. **Form the maintainer-collective entity.** Probably a non-profit
   foundation registered in a jurisdiction friendly to open-source
   stewardship and Inviolata-grade privacy commitments (Software Freedom
   Conservancy, Open Collective fiscal sponsorship, or a freshly-
   incorporated foundation in the US with a Switzerland or Iceland
   secondary entity per [`MANIFESTO.md`](./MANIFESTO.md) jurisdictional
   posture — to be decided in consultation with counsel).
2. **File priority-jurisdiction trademarks** for the most
   identity-critical marks: *HONEST Software Manifesto*, *HONEST
   License*, *HONEST-compliant*, *Inviolata*. US first, then EU/UK/India.
3. **Commission the HONEST mark/logo design** under a CC BY 4.0 release
   (graphic license matching the Manifesto's text license). Open call to
   designers in the movement, with a small honorarium.
4. **Publish the formal Trademark Use Guidelines** v1.0, replacing this
   placeholder, once the entity exists and the marks are filed.
5. **Establish a lightweight enforcement protocol**: 30-day cure on
   accidental misuse (typos, honest mistakes); cease-and-desist on
   willful misuse. No litigation as a first resort; reputation is the
   primary enforcement mechanism for this kind of movement.

## 9. How to ask for permission

**Currently:** open an issue at
[github.com/honestsoftware-org/honestsoftware/issues](https://github.com/honestsoftware-org/honestsoftware/issues)
with the label `trademark-request` (label to be added). Describe the
intended use, the operator, the product, and link to the operator's
Filial Duty Statement.

**Eventually (post-entity-formation):** a dedicated email address and a
short standardized form. Replied within 30 days; a permitted-use list
will be published transparently.

The bias is toward permitting good-faith use. A movement that gates its
own identity behind unanswerable bureaucracy defeats its own purpose.

## 10. What this document is not

- **Not a registered trademark filing.** No marks have been filed.
  Anyone reading this who has prior art on any of the listed marks
  please open an issue; we will adjust rather than collide.
- **Not a litigation threat.** The HONEST movement intends to use
  reputation, public commitment, and the cure-and-termination structure
  of the License as its primary enforcement mechanisms. Trademark
  litigation is a last resort, used only against willful, sustained,
  bad-faith capture.
- **Not final.** This is a **placeholder v0.1**. It will be superseded
  by formal Trademark Use Guidelines once an entity exists to hold the
  marks. Until then, this document represents the maintainer's
  good-faith intent.
- **Not a barrier to honest participation.** Re-read §3 if any of the
  above sounds gatekeeping. The bias is firmly toward permitting
  factual, descriptive, editorial, and non-commercial use without any
  permission step at all.
- **Not the product-level trademark policy for VaultAI or any other
  reference implementation.** Those are governed separately, by their
  respective project repositories. See for example
  [VaultAI's TRADEMARK.md](https://github.com/sgireddy/VaultAI/blob/main/TRADEMARK.md).
